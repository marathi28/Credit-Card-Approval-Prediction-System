The Problem

Manual review doesn't scale.

A significant share of applications are rejected for reasons like high existing loan balances, insufficient income, or excessive credit inquiries — but reviewing each one by hand is slow and inconsistent. This project automates the decision by training a predictive model on historical applicant outcomes, then serving real-time predictions through this web interface.

Applied Scenarios

Where this runs in practice

Scenario 01

Automated Application Screening

A credit analyst enters an applicant's income type, employment duration, and credit history. The model returns an instant approve/reject signal, letting the analyst prioritize applications that need deeper human review.

Scenario 02

High-Risk Identification & Compliance

A compliance officer batch-screens applicants with past-due loan records. Multi-class payment status codes are engineered into binary risk labels so high-risk applicants are clearly flagged as ineligible.

Scenario 03

Portfolio Risk Review

Risk teams re-run the model against segments of the existing portfolio to catch applicants whose financial profile has drifted into higher-risk territory since approval.

Scenario 04

Customer Self-Service Eligibility Check

A prospective customer enters their own details before submitting a formal application. The system instantly estimates approval likelihood, reducing unnecessary rejections and wasted paperwork.
